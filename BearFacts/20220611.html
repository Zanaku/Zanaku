Saturday the 11th of June 2022 - Bears sleep for a vast majority of the adult lives. Known as hibernation it is believed that bears spend the winter months sleeping to lower their metabolism and go into a low state of consciousness to survive the harsher months without needing to forage for food sources that would be in low supply. This is untrue. Bears actually hibernate because they feel like it. Studies show some Bears have never actually woken up and actually exist in a dream state.
